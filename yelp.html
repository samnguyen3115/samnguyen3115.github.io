Yelp Dataset Challenge

Predicting Business Closure and Key Attributes

About the Project

This project addresses two critical challenges in business analysis using the Yelp dataset: predicting business closure and predicting key business attributes (such as price range, outdoor seating, and takeout availability). The analysis is built upon a substantial subset of the Yelp dataset consisting of approximately 150,000 businesses, 6.9 million reviews, and 200,000 pictures.

Predicting whether a business is active or closed is formulated as a binary classification problem. Collaborative filtering techniques (Matrix Factorization / SVD) are leveraged to predict business amenities and attributes based on user-business interactions, helping customers find businesses that fit their preferences.

Methodology & Models

  • Business Closure Classification - Trained and evaluated three model structures (Logistic Regression, Random Forests, and Gradient Boosting via XGBoost) to classify if a business is active or closed.
  • Feature Engineering - Extracted features across business details (categories, location, stars), review sentiment analytics, and user metrics (check-in frequency, ratio of reviews from elite users).
  • Attribute Recommendation (SVD) - Formulated amenity prediction as a recommendation system, treating business attributes as ratings and user reviews as interactions to predict price range, seating, and takeout.
  • Evaluation Rigor - Evaluated classification models using Accuracy, Precision, Recall, and F1-score; SVD predictions were validated using Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE).

Results & Analysis

1. Business Closure Prediction Results:

Model Accuracy Precision Recall F1-score
Logistic Regression 0.812 0.756 0.623 0.683
Random Forest 0.854 0.812 0.734 0.771
XGBoost 0.878 0.845 0.789 0.816

2. Attribute Prediction Results (RMSE):

Predicted Attribute RMSE
Price Range 0.654
Outdoor Seating 0.421
Takeout Availability 0.389

XGBoost outperformed the other classifiers, achieving 87.8% accuracy. Key contributors to the prediction model included the average stars of a business, the check-in frequency, review sentiment, and the proportion of elite reviewers. The collaborative filtering model achieved accurate predictions with RMSE as low as 0.389.
